# Conversion Rules

## Core Principle
Authority must lead to action. The Monolith's silent power must guide the user toward clear production decisions.

## CTA Rules
- **One Primary Action**: Every section must have one dominant primary action.
- **Intentionality**: CTAs must be specific and commanding. Avoid generic "Learn More".
- **Clarity over Ambiguity**: No vague links. Every button must describe the destination or the outcome.

## Messaging Rules
- **Technical ≠ Abstract**: Technical descriptions must always serve a business or operational "Why?".
- **Value-First**: Every functional block must answer: "What happens next?" or "Why does this matter to my facility?".

## Flow Rules
- **Directional Pathing**: Every page must act as a corridor guiding the user forward.
- **No Dead Ends**: Informational sections must end with a transition to the next stage of the funnel.
- **Action over Reading**: Minimize passive observation; encourage system interaction.

## Hierarchy of Actions
1. **Primary (Conversion)**: "Start Audit", "Open App", "Request Demo".
2. **Secondary (Exploration)**: "View Specs", "Examine Logic", "Platform Overview".
3. **Passive (Reading)**: References, Audit Logs, Metadata.

## Forbidden
- **Unclear CTA**: No buttons without a clearly defined result.
- **Competing CTAs**: Avoid two buttons with the same visual weight in the same view.
- **Passive Pages**: No page should exist simply to "inform" without providing a next step.
